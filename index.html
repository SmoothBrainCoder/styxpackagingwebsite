The Problem

A cheap bag undoes an expensive impression.

You've invested in your space, your branding, your service. Then the moment your customer leaves, all of it is carried out in packaging that quietly says average. The handle digs in. The bag creases on the walk home. It's in the bin before they get there, and so is the impression you worked to build.

The brown-bag default

What most small brands settle for

  • Thin kraft that creases and tears
  • Twisted-paper handles that cut into the hand
  • One finish, one colour, no point of difference
  • Expensive per-unit on the small runs you actually need
  • Forgettable, indistinguishable from every other shop
The Styx standard

What your brand deserves
to be carried in

  • Heavyweight, structured stock that holds its shape
  • Cotton-rope, ribbon or die-cut handles, finished by hand
  • Foil, emboss, spot UV and soft-touch, specified to your brand
  • Accessible runs without the supplier minimums
  • A bag the customer keeps, reuses, and remembers you by
